# How to Vet a Remote Filipino Bookkeeper (2025 Guide)

Most companies that have a bad experience outsourcing bookkeeping to the Philippines did not have a bad experience with Filipino professionals. They had a bad vetting process. Knowing **how to vet a remote Filipino bookkeeper** properly, before you extend an offer, is the difference between a long-term finance hire who runs your books autonomously and a costly restart three months in. This guide gives you a practical, step-by-step framework used by operators who have made this hire successfully.

---

## How to Vet a Remote Filipino Bookkeeper: The Core Framework

Vetting a bookkeeper remotely requires more structure than an in-office hire because you cannot rely on informal observation. Every signal has to come through deliberate screening. The framework below covers four layers: skills verification, scenario testing, soft-skills assessment, and reference checks.

### Layer 1: Resume and Credentials Review

Start with the basics, but go deeper than most hiring managers do.

- **Years of experience**: For a senior role, require a minimum of five years in bookkeeping or accounting. Candidates with three years often know the tools but lack the judgment to catch problems independently.
- **Software proficiency**: QuickBooks Online, Xero, and Bill.com are table stakes for US-market bookkeepers. Ask specifically which version they have used and for how long. "Familiar with QuickBooks" is not the same as "managed month-end close in QBO for 12 entities."
- **Industry exposure**: A bookkeeper who has worked in e-commerce understands inventory-impacted cost of goods sold. One who has only done service businesses may struggle with Shopify reconciliation or Amazon Seller Central payouts. Match their background to your business model.
- **CPA or CPA-adjacent credentials**: A Filipino CPA (Certified Public Accountant) has passed a national board exam widely considered one of the most rigorous in Asia. It is not the same credential as a US CPA, but it signals serious technical training. If you are hiring for a bookkeeper role, a CPA is not required, but a bookkeeper who has worked under a CPA-supervised environment typically has stronger discipline. See our post on [Filipino CPA qualifications for US companies](/blog/filipino-cpa-qualifications-for-us-companies) for a full credential breakdown.

### Layer 2: A Skills-Based Scenario Test

Resumes tell you what someone has done. A scenario test tells you how they think. This is the step most companies skip, and it is the most predictive layer of the entire vetting process.

Give candidates a practical test that mirrors your actual workflow. Effective test formats include:

- A messy trial balance with two classification errors and ask them to identify what is wrong
- A bank reconciliation with three unmatched transactions and ask them to explain how they would resolve each
- A short written prompt: "A client's accounts payable balance increased 40% month-over-month but revenue was flat. What do you look at first?"

You are not looking for perfect answers. You are looking for the reasoning process. A senior bookkeeper will ask clarifying questions before answering. A junior one will guess.

Limit the test to 30 to 45 minutes of actual work. Anything longer signals poor respect for the candidate's time and will push strong candidates toward other opportunities.

### Layer 3: The Video Interview

For a remote hire, the video interview is your only window into communication style and professionalism. Use it strategically.

**Key Filipino bookkeeper interview questions to ask:**

- "Walk me through how you handle month-end close. What does your checklist look like?"
- "Describe a time you caught an error that was not yours to catch. What did you do?"
- "How do you communicate a cash flow concern to a business owner who is not financially fluent?"
- "We are a US-based business. What do you know about the differences between Philippine accounting standards and US GAAP?"
- "What does your home office setup look like, and what is your backup if your internet goes down?"

The last two questions matter more than they seem. Awareness of US GAAP versus PFRS (Philippine Financial Reporting Standards) gaps shows that the candidate understands they are working in a different regulatory context. The internet backup question is a practical resilience check. Every senior remote professional in the Philippines should have an answer ready.

**Red flags in the interview:**

- Vague answers about their own processes ("I just follow what the client needs")
- Inability to articulate a reconciliation process step by step
- No mention of documentation, checklists, or audit trails
- Hesitation when asked about tools they listed on their resume

## Building a Remote Bookkeeper Skills Checklist

Before you finalize a hire, run through this checklist. It covers the non-negotiables for a senior remote bookkeeper supporting a US or EU company.

**Technical skills:**
- Proficiency in at least one primary accounting platform (QBO, Xero, or NetSuite)
- Experience with bank feeds, reconciliation, and accrual-basis accounting
- Familiarity with US tax terminology, even if they are not preparing returns (1099s, W-9 collection, sales tax basics)
- Comfort with payroll platforms (Gusto, ADP, or similar) if payroll is in scope
- Spreadsheet fluency in Google Sheets or Excel, including pivot tables and VLOOKUP or XLOOKUP

**Process and communication skills:**
- Documented month-end close process (ask for a sample checklist or SOP)
- Proactive communication style, not just responsive
- Experience preparing reports for non-accountant stakeholders
- Ability to work overlap hours with your team (most Manila-based professionals can cover 8 AM to 12 PM US Eastern without issue)

**Infrastructure:**
- Dedicated home office with a minimum of 25 Mbps stable internet
- Backup internet connection (pocket WiFi or secondary ISP)
- Working laptop with a webcam for regular video check-ins

## Onboarding an Offshore Bookkeeper from the Philippines

A strong hire becomes a weak hire without structured onboarding. The first 30 days set the pattern for everything that follows.

### Week 1: Access and Orientation

Do not give full system access on day one. Start with read-only access to your accounting platform, your chart of accounts, and any relevant historical reports. Ask them to review the last three months of transactions and write back a short summary of what they observed. This tells you immediately how they read financial data and whether they understand your business.

### Weeks 2 to 4: Supervised Execution

Assign one recurring task (typically bank reconciliation) and review their output before it is finalized. Give direct, specific feedback. Senior professionals respond well to precise correction and poorly to vague directives like "make it cleaner."

Establish a weekly check-in cadence: a 30-minute video call to review open items, blockers, and upcoming deadlines. This is not a status meeting. It is your quality control mechanism in the early phase.

### Compliance Considerations for US Companies

If you are engaging a Filipino bookkeeper as an independent contractor rather than through an employer of record (EOR), keep a few things in mind:

- The Philippines Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) taxes Filipino professionals on Philippine-sourced income. Compliance is their responsibility, but you should confirm they understand their tax obligations.
- If you are a US company, you typically do not withhold US taxes for foreign contractors. However, you may need to collect a Form W-8BEN for US tax purposes if they receive US-sourced payments.
- For higher-trust roles that will handle payment approvals or wire transfers, consider requiring a signed NDA and data processing agreement before granting access.

## Key Takeaways

- The failure mode in outsourcing bookkeeping to the Philippines is almost always a vetting failure, not a talent failure.
- A four-layer process (credentials review, scenario test, structured interview, reference check) filters out candidates who look good on paper but cannot perform independently.
- Match the candidate's industry background to your business model. E-commerce bookkeeping and service-business bookkeeping require different instincts.
- Onboarding structure in the first 30 days determines whether the hire becomes autonomous or dependent.
- Senior Filipino bookkeepers with US-market experience typically expect $1,200 to $2,200 per month for full-time remote roles, depending on experience level and scope.
