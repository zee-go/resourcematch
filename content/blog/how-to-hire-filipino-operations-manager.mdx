## How to Find and Define the Right Role

"Operations manager" is one of the most overloaded titles in remote hiring. It covers at least four distinct profiles:

- **E-commerce operations manager**: Amazon Seller Central or Shopify backend, inventory replenishment, 3PL coordination, fulfillment SLAs
- **Business process operations manager**: Internal workflows, SOPs, cross-functional coordination, vendor relationships
- **Finance operations manager**: Cash flow visibility, AP/AR oversight, reporting to founders or CFOs
- **Customer and team operations manager**: Team performance, shift scheduling, QA, CS escalations

These roles require different skill sets and different compensation. A candidate who excels at Shopify fulfillment may have no business near your financial reporting stack.

**Define the role around outputs, not tasks.** Instead of "manage daily operations," write: "Own inventory forecasting for 400 SKUs, reduce stockout rate from 8% to under 3%, and deliver weekly performance reports to the COO."

### What Senior Experience Looks Like

For a 5-10+ year Filipino operations manager, expect:

- Direct team management (typically 5-20 people across remote and in-country staff)
- Ownership of a P&L segment or operational budget
- Specific tool experience relevant to your stack (Asana, NetSuite, Jungle Scout, Shopify)
- Measurable process improvement outcomes, not just "managed operations"
- Prior US or EU client experience in a timezone-overlapping arrangement

## The Four-Stage Vetting Process

Most companies run a 30-minute Zoom call, check candidate-provided references, and make an offer. That approach fails more often than hiring managers want to admit. Structured vetting covers four dimensions.

**Stage 1: Resume Analysis.** Look for scope indicators (team size, budget ownership), vertical specificity, career progression logic, and tool fluency consistent with claimed seniority.

**Stage 2: Scenario Assessment.** Send a written scenario relevant to your business. Example: "Our Amazon FBA inventory is forecasted manually in a spreadsheet. We've had two stockouts costing $40,000. Walk us through how you'd rebuild this process." Give 48-72 hours. Strong candidates structure their answer with clear phases and reference real tools.

**Stage 3: Structured Video Interview.** Cover a specific failure they recovered from, a time they pushed back on an executive, and a cross-functional project. Ask them to walk through how they actually use a tool, not just whether they know it.

**Stage 4: Reference Verification.** Call references (don't email). Try to reach a direct manager independently, not just the candidate's curated list. Ask: "Would you hire them again, and if not, why not?"

## Salary Benchmarks

Compensation for a senior Filipino operations manager (fully remote, full-time):

- **E-commerce ops (Shopify/Amazon, 5-8 years)**: $1,800-$2,800/month
- **Business process / team ops (7-10 years)**: $2,000-$3,200/month
- **Finance-adjacent ops with FP&A exposure**: $2,500-$3,800/month
- **Senior director-level (10+ years)**: $3,500-$5,000/month

Add 10-15% for EOR arrangements. For context on how these economics compare, see our [Filipino vs. US accountant full cost comparison](/blog/cost-of-hiring-filipino-accountant-vs-us).

**Do not underprice this role.** A senior ops manager hired at $1,200/month when the market rate is $2,500 will leave within six months. The cost of a failed senior hire (re-recruitment, onboarding time, operational disruption) typically exceeds 3-4 months of salary.

## Structuring the First 90 Days

**Days 1-30:** Provide documented access to every tool and process. Assign a specific audit task ("document the current state of our inventory management and identify the three biggest risks") rather than "get settled." Daily async check-ins for the first two weeks.

**Days 31-60:** Hand off one process with clear success metrics. Weekly 30-minute video sync focused on blockers. Evaluate their written communication as a leading indicator.

**Days 61-90:** They should be running the function independently. Formal review: outcomes achieved, gaps, and the KPI framework going forward.

## Red Flags

- Cannot describe a specific tool at operational depth (only marketing-level descriptions)
- Vague on team size and scope ("I managed a team" with no specifics)
- Overpromises on availability without asking about workload expectations
- Asks nothing about your business, challenges, or team structure
- Written scenario response is sharp but they can't explain their own thinking on a call

## Key Takeaways

- Define the role around measurable outputs before recruiting. "Operations manager" covers four distinct profiles.
- Scenario-based assessment is the most predictive vetting stage. A 48-hour written challenge reveals operational thinking better than any interview.
- Market rate for a senior Filipino ops manager is $1,800-$3,800/month. Underpaying creates churn.
- The first 90 days need structured ownership transfer, not passive observation.
- Pre-vetted talent pools reduce time-to-hire and filter out unqualified candidates before you invest screening time.
