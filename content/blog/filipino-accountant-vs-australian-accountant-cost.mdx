# Filipino vs Australian Accountant: Full Cost Breakdown

The true cost comparison between a **Filipino accountant vs an Australian accountant** is more significant than most AU business owners expect, and it goes well beyond the salary line. When you factor in superannuation, annual leave loading, payroll tax, office overhead, and recruitment fees, hiring a senior accountant locally can cost your business $120,000 AUD or more per year. A senior Filipino accountant with equivalent experience and Xero or MYOB proficiency typically costs $28,000 to $45,000 AUD annually, all-in. This breakdown covers every cost layer so you can make a fully informed decision.

## The Filipino Accountant vs Australian Accountant Cost Gap, Fully Itemised

Most cost comparisons stop at base salary. That undersells the real picture. Below is a complete breakdown across both hiring options for a mid-to-senior level accountant with five or more years of experience.

### Australian Accountant: True Annual Cost

| Cost Component | Annual Estimate (AUD) |
|---|---|
| Base salary (mid-senior) | $80,000 – $105,000 |
| Superannuation (11.5%) | $9,200 – $12,075 |
| Annual leave loading (17.5%) | $2,400 – $3,200 |
| Payroll tax (state-dependent, ~4.85%) | $3,880 – $5,093 |
| Workers' compensation insurance | $800 – $1,500 |
| Recruitment fee (15–20% of salary) | $12,000 – $21,000 (one-off) |
| Office space / equipment / software | $8,000 – $15,000 |
| Sick leave, long service leave provisions | $3,000 – $5,000 |
| **Total Year-One Cost** | **$119,280 – $167,868** |

Note: Payroll tax thresholds vary by state. Victorian businesses pay from $900K total wage bill; NSW from $1.2M. Small businesses near or above those thresholds absorb the full rate.

### Senior Filipino Accountant: True Annual Cost

| Cost Component | Annual Estimate (AUD) |
|---|---|
| Base contractor rate | $24,000 – $42,000 |
| Platform access / vetting fee | $300 – $600 (one-off) |
| Software licences (Xero, MYOB) | $0 – $1,200 (often shared) |
| Equipment allowance (optional) | $500 – $1,000 |
| Superannuation | Not applicable (offshore contractor) |
| Payroll tax | Not applicable |
| Annual leave loading | Not applicable |
| **Total Annual Cost** | **$24,800 – $44,800** |

The savings range from **$75,000 to $123,000 AUD per year** for a single hire. For a growing SME or mid-market firm running lean, that is the difference between hiring one local accountant and funding two or three senior offshore specialists across different functions.

## What You Are Actually Buying With a Senior Filipino Accountant

This is where the comparison gets more nuanced, and where poorly-run offshore hiring fails. If you hire a junior virtual assistant and call them an accountant, you will get junior output. The case for offshore accounting only holds when you hire at the right seniority level.

Senior Filipino accountants with five to ten-plus years of experience regularly handle:

- **BAS lodgment preparation** and GST reconciliation under supervision of a registered BAS agent or CA
- **PAYG withholding schedules** and Single Touch Payroll (STP) submissions via Xero Payroll or MYOB
- **Xero and MYOB at an advanced level**, including payroll, job costing, and multi-entity consolidation
- **Taxable Payments Annual Report (TPAR)** preparation for construction, cleaning, and courier businesses
- **Management accounts and cash flow reporting** aligned to Australian financial year conventions
- Accounts payable and receivable at volume, bank reconciliations, and month-end close

One important compliance note: BAS lodgment with the ATO must be signed off by a registered BAS agent or tax agent. Your offshore accountant handles the preparation, data entry, reconciliation, and reporting. The final lodgment sits with your Australian-registered advisor or in-house CA. This division of labour is standard practice and does not reduce the value of the offshore role.

## The Time Zone Advantage Australian Businesses Have

Australia has a structural advantage over every other major English-speaking market when it comes to working with Manila-based professionals.

**AEST is only 2 to 3 hours ahead of Philippine Standard Time.** During Australian business hours, a Manila-based accountant is already at their desk and working. This means:

- Morning standups and daily check-ins happen in real time
- Urgent reconciliations or same-day reporting requests are handled within normal hours
- No reliance on asynchronous handoffs or overnight turnaround
- End-of-day review by your AU team aligns with late-afternoon Manila time

This is a material operational difference compared to UK or US businesses, who must either accept a time-shifted workflow or pay a premium for professionals working outside standard Manila hours. Australian businesses get near real-time collaboration as the default.
