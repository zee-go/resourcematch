The Philippines has become the default outsourcing market for AU accounting teams, not because it is cheap, but because the talent is genuinely strong. The time zone advantage is real and underappreciated. Manila (PHT) sits just 2 to 3 hours behind AEST. ### Do Filipino bookkeepers actually understand Australian BAS and GST requirements?

Senior candidates with AU client experience do, yes. The Philippines has a large cohort of bookkeepers who have worked inside Australian-facing accounting firms, BPOs, or directly with AU clients for 5 or more years. ### Is superannuation applicable when I hire a Filipino contractor?

No. Superannuation obligations under Australian law apply to employees and, in some cases, contractors working under specific arrangements on Australian soil. A Filipino professional working remotely from the Philippines as an independent contractor falls outside the Superannuation Guarantee scope. You should confirm your specific arrangement with your accountant or a Philippines-familiar employment lawyer, but this is standard practice across thousands of AU-Philippines offshore arrangements.

### What is the typical engagement model, contractor or employee?

Most Australian businesses engage Filipino remote professionals as independent contractors. The professional invoices monthly, typically in USD or AUD, and manages their own Philippine tax obligations. This is the standard model. Some businesses use a Philippine Employer of Record (EOR) for more structured arrangements.
